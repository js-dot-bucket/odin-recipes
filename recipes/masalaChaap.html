Masala Chaap

Description

Masala Chaap in gravy is a flavorful North Indian dish that features succulent chaap (made from textured soy protein or soya chunks) cooked in a rich, aromatic, and creamy spiced gravy. The gravy is typically made from a blend of onions, tomatoes, cream, and a mix of traditional spices like garam masala, red chili powder, turmeric, and ginger-garlic paste. This dish offers a delightful balance of spicy, creamy, and tangy flavors, making it a perfect indulgence for vegetarians and food lovers seeking a hearty main course.

This dish is often garnished with fresh coriander leaves and sometimes a drizzle of cream or butter, enhancing its sumptuous look and taste. Masala chaap gravy pairs well with Indian breads like naan, roti, or paratha, as well as steamed rice, providing a wholesome and satisfying meal. Its unique texture and robust flavors make it a popular choice in Indian restaurants and at festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • 10 sticks soya chaap
  • ½ cup curd
  • 1-2 tsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1-2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 2 onions
  • 4 tomatoes
  • Spices: bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, cumin seeds, kasuri methi, garam masala. Also, oil and fresh coriander leaves for cooking and garnish.

Steps

  1. Marinate soya chaap sticks with yogurt, turmeric, red chili powder, ginger garlic paste, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and salt. Set aside for 30 minutes.
  2. Shallow fry or grill the marinated chaap sticks until golden and set aside.
  3. In a pan, heat oil and add whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, cumin seeds). Then sauté finely chopped onions until golden brown.
  4. Add tomato puree, turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and cook until oil separates. Add water and kasuri methi, simmer the gravy.
  5. Add fried chaap sticks to the gravy, cook for 5-7 minutes, garnish with fresh coriander leaves, and serve hot.
